About the T1D Spatial Atlas

An open research platform built to advance understanding of type 1 diabetes progression using spatial transcriptomics at single-cell resolution.

About the Project

The T1D Spatial Atlas is a research initiative combining CosMX (NanoString) spatial transcriptomics with AI-powered analysis to map the cellular and molecular landscape of human pancreatic tissue across four stages of type 1 diabetes (T1D) progression.

By profiling over 1.1 million cells from 20 donors at single-cell resolution with preserved spatial coordinates, this atlas enables researchers to understand how the immune microenvironment changes during insulitis and beta cell loss.

Privacy & Ethics

All human tissue samples were collected under IRB-approved protocols with informed donor consent. Data has been de-identified and is shared in accordance with NIH data sharing policies. Controlled-access data is available through the NIDDK Central Repository.

This platform does not collect personal user data beyond anonymized usage analytics.
